 Trade-a-Tree Program





Event Details:After Christmas each year, trade in your old tree for a free seedling! The Cayuga County Trade-a-Tree program offers residents an opportunity to recycle their Christmas tree in exchange for a conifer seedling. It's fast, easy, and free!The Cayuga County Soil and Water Conservation District will take your old tree to chip it into mulch and provide you with a certificate for a tree seedling to be picked up in the spring. Certificates are redeemable at the Annual Conservation District Spring Tree & Shrub Sale on May 8th, 2026. This year, anyone who redeems their certificate at the sale will receive a free White Spruce seedling!Location:Trees will be accepted at the Natural Resource Center, 7413 County House Road, Auburn, NY.Dates and Times: Trees will be accepted during the following hours:December 26, 2025, from 8 AM – 3:30 PM. December 29-30, 2025, from 8 AM – 3:30 PM. December 31, 2025, from 8 AM – 12 PM. January 2, 2026, from 8 AM – 3:30 PM. January 5-9, 2026, from 8 AM - 3:30 PM. January 12-16, 2026, from 8 AM – 3:30 PM. Guidelines:  Trees must be free of decorations, tinsel, wraps or any other foreign objects. Additional Information: For more information, please visit the Cayuga County Soil and Water Conservation District website or call them at 315-252-4171, ext. 4. 





 CCA Styrofoam Collection Event





Event DetailsCayuga Climate Action, a community volunteer group, is offering a unique chance for residents to recycle clean, white polystyrene packaging foam. The main event will be held on January 10th, 2026 but some drop-off locations have varying open hours. Foam packaging takes up significant space in household trash and cannot be placed in curbside recycling bins. Through a partnership with a Syracuse-based recycler, residents can now divert this material responsibly during a limited, one-time collection period.What You Can BringClean, white polystyrene packaging foam (the type used to protect appliances and electronics)Clean, white foam coolersNO Packing Peanuts, Multi-colored foam, or Foam that has come into contact with food, including trays and clamshellsLocationsAuburn - 1533 Clark Street Rd (Riester's Main Office)Open on January 10th from 10AM - 1PMWeedsport - 2601 Earl St (DPW Office)Open every Thursday from 9AM - 3PM and Saturday from 7AM - 3PM until January 10thLedyard - 2530 NY-34B (Southern Cayuga Instant Aid)  Open on January 10th from 10AM - PMLansingContact [email protected] to schedule a drop-offFor more information, please contact [email protected]. 





 Spring Electronics Recycling Event





Event Details:The 2025 event was held on March 22nd and resulted in the collection of over 57,000 pounds of electronic materials to be reused or recycled. If you still have electronics that you need to get rid of, please visit our "What To Do I Do With..?" page for more information. 





 Tire Collection Event





2025 Tire Collection EventThe 2025 event was held on Saturday, June 7th, and resulted in the collection of 4,021 used tires - approximately 130,000 pounds of materials to be reused or recycled. Tires collected at this year's event will either be used as raw materials in steel production at NUCOR Steel Auburn or shredded and recycled for other purposes. If you still have tires that you need to get rid of, please visit our "What To Do I Do With..?" page for more information. 





 Fall Electronics Recycling Event





Event DetailsThe 2025 event was held on October 18th and resulted in the collection of over 45,000 pounds of electronic materials to be reused or recycled. If you still have electronics that you need to get rid of, please visit our "What To Do I Do With..?" page for more information. 





 Household Hazardous Waste Recycling Event





Event Details:The 2025 event was held on October 25th and resulted in the collection of approximately 31,000 pounds of hazardous household materials to be properly disposed of or recycled. If you still have hazardous waste materials that you need to get rid of, please visit our "What To Do I Do With..?" page for more information.
