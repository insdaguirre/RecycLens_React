Clothing and Textiles		






Textiles including clothing, footwear, belts, hats, handbags, drapes, towels, sheets, and other linens can be reused or recycled. It is estimated that the average person throws away 81 pounds of clothing each year even though just about everything (regardless of condition) can be reclaimed. Donations and Recycling OpportunitiesWhat to Donate:Even if items are torn, stained, missing buttons, missing zippers, etc, they can still be recycled so long as they are dry and free of oils, greases, and odors. Clothing - Shirts, pants, jackets, suits, hats, belts, ties, gloves, scarves, socks (even single ones), undergarments, handbags, and backpacks. Footwear - Shoes, sandals, sneakers, cleats, boots, flip-flops, and slippers. Household Textiles - Curtains, drapes, sheets, blankets, comforters, towels, table linens, pillows, stuffed dolls and animals. Where to Donate:You can bring your reusable and recyclable clothing to: Local Charities and Thrift StoresTextile Drop-off Bins and ShedsClick Here to find a recovery location near you!What Happens to the Materials:Nearly 100% of donated textiles are reused or recycled in New York State.~45% are reused as clothing~20% are recycled into fibers~30% are reused as wiping cloths
