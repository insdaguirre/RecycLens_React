            Recycling in St. Lawrence County 
 
St. Lawrence County Solid Waste Department currently utilizes a Single Stream or Zero Sort 
recycling process.  The County’s transfer stations have drop off points for all approved 
recyclables. 
 
 
Single Stream or Zero Sort Recycling: All approved recyclables can be placed in the 
same container. 
 Glass 
 
o Bottles & Jars 
o Clear (flint), Green (emerald) & Brown (amber) 
 
 Metal 
 
o “Tin” food cans 
o Aluminum cans, foil & pans 
o Aerosol spray cans (must be empty / spent) 
o Bottle & Jar caps and lids 
o Other small metal items 
 
 Plastic 
 
o Pourable plastic bottles & jugs 
o Small open-mouthed containers (yogurt, margarine, sour cream, etc.), lids must be 
removed, but are recyclable 
o Look for item 1-7 on the bottom 
 
 Cartons 
 
o Juice, Milk, Soy, Broth & Soup cartons 
o Aseptic Packaging – small, medium & large juice pouches, boxes, cartons, etc. 
 
 
 Clean Paper and Cardboard 
 
o Newspaper and inserts 
o Magazine & Catalogs 
o Paper grocery bags 
o “Junk mail” & envelopes 
o Computer & Office paper 
o Books & publications, including phone books and paperbacks (no hard covers) 
o Cereal boxes & other foods boxes (must be clean) 
 
 
How to Recycle 
 
 Rinse away all residue 
 Remove and recycle metal caps & lids 
 It is not necessary to remove labels or metal & plastic rings 
 Crush larger cans, plastic bottles & jugs to the best of your ability 
 Do not crush glass bottles & jars 
 Breakdown / flatten all boxes 
 Cut larger pieces of cardboard into 2’ x 2’ pieces & tie in manageable bundles
