Food Scraps
These items don’t belong in your curbside bin, but can be brought directly to drop 
off areas at the Recycling and Solid Waste Center. 
Batteries 
Rechargeable, car batteries (lead-
acid), phone, laptop, cordless tool, 
button batteries accepted—kindly tape 
terminals for safety
Electronics 
Computers & accessories, office 
electronics, TVs, mobile devices, 
entertainment equipment, wall & 
battery powered devices
Glass 
Clean glass bottles and jars -  
no lids or caps
Scrap Metal 
Metal appliances and other metal 
objects
Textile Donations 
Clothing, footwear, linens, and other 
textiles
Yard Waste 
Leaves, grass clippings, branches, and 
brush - permits and fees required
For a full list of drop off areas, acceptable materials, fees, and restrictions visit 
RecycleTompkins.org. Other drop off areas include Fats Oils and Grease, Food 
Scraps, Appliances with Refrigerants, Propane Tanks, Rigid Plastics, and Tires.
Additional Recyclables for Drop Off 
Recycling collection is provided at the curb for County residents every other week. 
Refer to the table below to find your pick-up day. The calendar below shows green 
shaded and non-shaded weeks through June 2026.
Set out recycling by 6:00 am on your scheduled day unless otherwise noted.  
Late set-outs will not be collected until the next scheduled pick-up day. Visit www.
recycletompkins.org for weather-related notifications and to sign up for automatic 
reminders via phone, email, or text. Residents can also bring their recyclables to the 
Recycling and Solid Waste Center at no charge.
